Design Choices

Why the architecture looks like this: minimal kernel + backends/tactics + certificates.

Kernel IR (UBH)

XOR/AND/CONST over GF(2) provide a minimal Boolean basis, with a direct polynomial interpretation and efficient hash-consing (structural compression).

Solving strategy

SAT + native XOR (linear GF(2) equations) avoids “blow-up” when encoding XOR into CNF and matches the structure of typical UBH circuits.

Schema engine / CEGAR

Quantifiers and generative rules are handled via lazy instantiation (CEGAR) to keep theories compact and avoid full grounding.

Deterministic front-end (CNL/DSL)

CNL/DSL are controlled: typing is mandatory and the vocabulary is strict (unknown symbol = error). Ambiguity is rejected, not guessed.

Fragments + certificates

The system separates “what problem do we have?” (Fragment) from “how do we solve it?” (Backend/Tactic). Any accepted result must be checkable (witness/certificate), and the “trusted” part remains small.
