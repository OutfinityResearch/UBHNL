The universal module: how you choose a method and keep results checkable.

1) Fragment + GoalKind = a normalized problem

The orchestrator receives a Fragment (which constraint class is involved) and a GoalKind (what you want), then chooses tactics/backends.

2) Backends are pluggable

SAT/SMT/QBF/IC3/PDR/MIP/… are interchangeable “engines”. UBHNL does not take them on faith: acceptance is filtered via checkable certificates.

3) Plan DAG and tactics

  • Lower-to-UBH: for bitvectors and finite constraints.
  • CEGAR: for schemas/abstractions (CEGAR).
  • IC3/PDR: for TS safety (IC3/PDR).
  • KC: compile once, query many (knowledge compilation).

4) UNKNOWN is part of the contract

If you do not obtain a witness/certificate within budget, return UNKNOWN. This is preferable to any unverifiable claim (decision).
