The Use Case: The Logistics Manager

Marco is a logistics manager for a global shipping company. His day is a mix of different problems:

  • Logical Rules: "If a truck is refrigerated, it can carry medicine."
  • Optimization: "Find the cheapest route that meets all delivery times."
  • Probability: "What is the chance of a delay due to the storm?"
No single solver is good at all of these. Marco needs a Conductor who can delegate each part of the problem to the right specialist.

The Orchestrator: The Brain behind the Plan

In UBHNL, the Orchestrator is the brain that manages complexity. It doesn't just throw everything at a SAT solver. Instead, it follows a sophisticated process:

  1. Fragment Detection: It looks at the problem and says: "This part is linear math, this part is simple logic, and this part is a search for a witness."
  2. The Plan: It builds a Plan DAG—a step-by-step strategy for how to solve the puzzle.
  3. Delegation: It sends the fragments to specialized Backends.

Fragments: The Building Blocks of Complexity

We call these specialized parts Fragments. By breaking a "Universal" problem into smaller, well-defined fragments, we can use the most efficient tools for each job. A simple logic puzzle doesn't need a supercomputer, but a complex optimization might. The Orchestrator knows the difference.

Type Lowering: The Universal Translation

How does the Orchestrator communicate with all these different specialists? Through Type Lowering. It takes high-level concepts (like "Trucks" and "Routes") and translates them into the minimal language of the UBH Kernel: Bits and Wires. This ensures that even though many specialists are involved, they all agree on the final "Ground Truth."

Current Limits & Future Plans

What we have: A flexible orchestrator that can route problems to SAT solvers and check their results.

What we're building: Smarter Planning. We are teaching the Orchestrator to estimate the "Difficulty" of a problem before it starts. This will allow it to choose better strategies and give Marco faster results for his logistics routes.
