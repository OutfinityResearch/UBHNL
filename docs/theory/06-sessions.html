The Use Case: The Lab Notebook

Meet David, a research scientist. He has a Textbook of established biological facts (Theory files). But today, he's in the lab, and he wants to try something new: "What if this specific protein is inhibited? How does the cell react?"

David doesn't want to rewrite the textbook. He just wants a Lab Notebook (a Session) where he can add temporary notes, run a simulation, and then decide whether to keep the results or throw them away.

The Ephemeral Workspace

In UBHNL, a Session is your personal, temporary workspace.

  • Theory Files are your "Ground Truth"—stable, reproducible, and stored on disk.
  • Sessions are where you learn new, transient facts or ask "What-if" questions.
When the session ends, the temporary facts disappear, but the stable Theory remains untouched.

Origins: No Fact Left Behind

Every time David adds a note to his notebook, the system attaches an Origin to it. An origin is like a digital fingerprint that says exactly where a piece of logic came from (e.g., "File A, Line 42" or "Interactive Input #5").

Why does this matter? Because if the system finds a contradiction, it can tell David: "Your experiment failed because the fact you just added in the notebook contradicts the textbook at Page 200." This makes debugging complex rules a breeze.

Caching: Standing on the Shoulders of Giants

Reasoning is hard work for a computer. To keep things fast, UBHNL uses Intelligent Caching. If David asks the same question twice, or if he only changes one tiny variable, the system recognizes that the rest of the "Textbook" hasn't changed. It reuses its previous calculations, giving David results in milliseconds instead of minutes.

What's next

What we have: A robust session system that tracks origins and ensures reproducibility.

What we're building: Collaboration Features. We want to make it easy for David to "Export" his successful lab notes and merge them back into the main Theory files, complete with all their proofs and origins intact.
