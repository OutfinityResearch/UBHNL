Theory 04 — Solving: SAT + XOR, certificate, UNKNOWN

How solve/prove works, and why certificates are part of the public contract.

1) solve()

solve() answers: “does there exist a model that satisfies the constraints?”. The only statuses are SAT, UNSAT, and UNKNOWN.

  • SAT: includes a checkable witness (model).
  • UNSAT: includes a checkable certificate.
  • UNKNOWN: budget exceeded, missing certificate, or an approximation method that cannot satisfy an “exact” goal.

2) prove(phi)

prove(phi) reduces to solve(T ∧ ¬phi). If the result is UNSAT (with a certificate), then phi is PROVED.

3) Why a SAT + XOR hybrid

AND gates are encoded into CNF using a linear-size Tseitin-style transformation, while XOR constraints form linear equations over GF(2). This motivates the SAT + native XOR decision.

4) Explain: unsat cores and origins

  • An unsat core (in terms of origins) helps explain and debug failures.
  • UNSAT is accepted only with a certificate; a core does not replace the certificate.
