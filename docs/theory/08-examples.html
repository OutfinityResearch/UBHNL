The Medical Detective

A patient walks into a clinic with a fever and a cough. The system doesn't just guess. It looks at its Medical Theory: "Flu causes fever and cough. A common cold causes a cough but rarely a high fever."

The system performs Abduction. It asks: "What disease, if present, would explain these observations?" The Result: The system suggests "Flu" as a likely witness, providing the logical chain that connects the disease to the symptoms.

The Chain of Trust

In a digital social network, Alice trusts Bob, and Bob trusts Charlie. Does Alice trust Charlie? The system applies a Transitivity Rule: "If X trusts Y, and Y trusts Z, then X trusts Z."

This is Deduction. The system follows the links, building a bridge of trust from Alice to Charlie. It doesn't just say "Yes"—it shows the path it took through the network.

The Ethical Switch

Imagine a safety system for a power plant. A switch cannot be both ON and OFF at the same time. This is a Consistency Constraint.

If a faulty sensor reports that the switch is both ON and OFF, the system detects a Conflict. It returns UNSAT and points exactly to the sensor data and the safety rule that are clashing. It prevents the system from making a dangerous decision based on contradictory data.

The Ancestor's Legacy

In a genealogical database, we know who everyone's parents are. But how do we find an ancestor from five generations ago? We use a Recursive Rule: "Your parent is your ancestor. Your parent's ancestor is also your ancestor."

The system uses Fixed-Point Reasoning to climb the family tree, connecting long-lost relatives through a rock-solid chain of "Parent-of" facts.
