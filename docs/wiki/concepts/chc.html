CHC (Constrained Horn Clauses)

A standard form for program verification: Horn rules with constraints in a background theory.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki SMT TS DS-010

Definition

A CHC is a rule of the form: 

(A1 ∧ A2 ∧ ... ∧ An ∧ C) ⇒ H

where Ai and H are predicates (possibly recursive), and C is a constraint in a background theory (e.g., BV/LIA).

In UBHNL

  • Frag_CHC is used for program verification, invariant synthesis, and recursive reasoning.
  • Typical backends: SMT-based CHC solvers or IC3-style recursion solvers.

Certificate

  • PROVED (safety): a model/invariant for predicates, checked with SMT.
  • DISPROVED: a checkable counterexample (trace/derivation).

Consequences

  • Integration requires a serializable “predicate invariant/model” artifact format.
  • Explanations can be narrative: which rule triggered which derivation (origins).

Relevant specs

  • DS-011 (certificate CHC)
