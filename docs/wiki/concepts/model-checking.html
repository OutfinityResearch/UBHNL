Model checking (Transition Systems)

Transition-system verification: traces as witnesses and invariants as proofs.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki IC3/PDR BMC CEGAR DS-010

Definition

A transition system is defined by: 

Init(s)      - the initial condition
Trans(s,s')  - the transition relation
Safe(s)      - the safety property

Two typical outcomes

  • DISPROVED: a trace s0 → s1 → ... → sk that starts in Init and reaches an unsafe state.
  • PROVED: an inductive invariant Inv(s) that implies Safe and is closed under Trans.

Certificate (checking invariants)

For an invariant Inv, the checker validates: 

1) Init(s) ⇒ Inv(s)
2) Inv(s) ∧ Trans(s,s') ⇒ Inv(s')
3) Inv(s) ⇒ Safe(s)

In UBHNL

  • Frag_TS is a distinct fragment; it can be solved with IC3/PDR or BMC (lowered to SAT/SMT/UBH).
  • Explanations come from the trace (counterexample) or from the invariant obligations.
