Origin (provenance / traceability)

A stable identifier for “where a constraint came from”: file/line/column plus a snippet.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki Session Unsat core DS-009

Definition

An Origin is metadata attached to a statement/constraint, used for explanation and audit. In the specs, the conceptual form is: 

Origin = ⟨sourceId, path?, line?, column?, snippet?, kind⟩
kind ∈ {DSL, CNL, API}

Why it matters

  • Unsat cores and explanations: when the theory is inconsistent, you want “which rules from which files”.
  • Reproducibility: same origins + same theory → same conclusions.
  • Debugging: “why was this instance chosen” (schema engine) can be linked back to the quantified rule’s origin.

Engineering consequences

  • Every backend/tactic must preserve origins at the artifact level (clause groups, equations, invariants).
  • Checkers can report “cores” in terms of origins, not internal identifiers.

Relevant specs

  • DS-009 (Origin, TheoryDoc, SessionState)
  • DS-002 (originId on clauses/equations)
