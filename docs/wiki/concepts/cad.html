CAD / Quantifier Elimination (reals)

Cylindrical Algebraic Decomposition: a classic method for NRA and quantifier elimination.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki Certificate DS-011

Definition

CAD is a technique to decide formulas in non-linear real arithmetic and to perform quantifier elimination, by decomposing the space into cells where the sign of polynomials is invariant.

Certificate

  • SAT: a witness point (concrete variable values), checkable by evaluating the constraints.
  • UNSAT: a CAD/QE certificate (when supported) or confirmation via a trusted QE checker.

In UBHNL

  • CAD appears as a backend for Frag_NRA (numeric/continuous).
  • The orchestrator treats it as native and requires certificates; lowering to UBH is the exception (only finite/bounded encodings).
