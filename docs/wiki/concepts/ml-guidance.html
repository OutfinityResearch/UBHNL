ML-guided proof search / premise selection

A heuristic layer to guide solvers; it is never a source of truth without checking.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki Certificate Orchestrator UNKNOWN

Definition

ML-guided methods use statistical/neural models to suggest decisions: literal ordering, premise selection, tactic choice, and related heuristics.

Policy in UBHNL

  • It is heuristic by design: it cannot produce acceptable results on its own.
  • It can only propose: “try lemma X” / “run backend Y”.
  • The final result must be validated by a checker (model/certificate/invariant).

Consequences

  • Auditability: keep logs of what ML suggested, but do not treat it as trusted.
  • If an ML-guided route does not produce certificates within budget, the status is UNKNOWN.
