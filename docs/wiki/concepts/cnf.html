CNF (Conjunctive Normal Form)

The standard SAT representation: a conjunction of clauses (disjunctions of literals).

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki Tseitin SAT DS-002

Definition

A CNF formula is a conjunction (AND) of clauses, where each clause is a disjunction (OR) of literals: 

(l11 ∨ l12 ∨ ...) ∧ (l21 ∨ l22 ∨ ...) ∧ ...

A literal is a Boolean variable or its negation.

Why it appears in UBHNL

  • The “AND” part of UBH is encoded into CNF using Tseitin (linear in the number of AND gates).
  • A SAT backend (CDCL) operates naturally on CNF; UBHNL uses SAT as a generic engine for non-linearity.

Important: equisatisfiable vs equivalent

  • Equivalent: exactly the same set of models over the original variables.
  • Equisatisfiable: same SAT/UNSAT answer, but it may introduce auxiliary variables (different model sets, preserved satisfiability).

Tseitin encodings are typically equisatisfiable, which is sufficient for solve/prove.
