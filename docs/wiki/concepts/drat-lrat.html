DRAT / LRAT (SAT UNSAT certificates)

Certificate formats for SAT UNSAT; checked deterministically in the trusted core.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki Certificate SAT DS-011

Idea

For a CNF formula, a solver can produce a proof trace that demonstrates UNSAT. A checker validates each step and confirms the final contradiction.

What the files contain (high level)

Both DRAT and LRAT are proof-trace formats that let a checker validate an UNSAT claim without re-solving the problem. The checker starts from the original CNF and processes a sequence of proof steps (typically clause additions and sometimes deletions). If the steps are valid and the empty clause is derived, the original CNF is UNSAT.

This matters for UBHNL because many fragments ultimately lower to SAT obligations. DRAT/LRAT gives UBHNL a standard, checkable “anchor” for accepting UNSAT.

DRAT vs LRAT (intuition)

  • DRAT: compact proof traces where the checker must do more work to justify each step.
  • LRAT: an “annotated” format where each step includes justification hints, making checking faster and more explicit.

How checking works (conceptual)

Why they appear in UBHNL

  • They are a practical standard anchor for accepting UNSAT in Boolean reasoning.
  • They allow SAT backends to be untrusted yet verifiable.
  • UBHNL can lower many claims to CNF UNSAT, and accepts only with DRAT/LRAT evidence.

In UBHNL

  • DRAT/LRAT are accepted as CertificateKind for SAT-level UNSAT obligations.
  • Certificates must be wrapped in the standard envelope and tied to a problemDigest (see DS-014).
  • If the checker is not available or the certificate is invalid, UBHNL must return UNKNOWN (not a guessed UNSAT).
