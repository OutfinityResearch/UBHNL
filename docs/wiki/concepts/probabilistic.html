Probabilistic inference

Probabilistic models: exact (WMC/KC) or approximate (BP/MCMC/SMC/VI) with explicit audit metadata.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki WMC/Counting KC UNKNOWN

Definition

Probabilistic inference means computing probabilities/conditionals in a model (e.g., a factor graph). In UBHNL, the discrete side connects naturally to WMC.

Exact vs approximate

  • Exact: via WMC/#SAT plus KC, with equivalence checks and audit.
  • Approximate: belief propagation / factor graphs, MCMC/SMC/VI; results must be labeled as probabilistic and accompanied by metadata (seeds, convergence diagnostics, bounds).

Consequences (UBHNL policy)

  • Approximate methods cannot return “UNSAT/PROVED” in the strict sense; they can produce estimates or guidance.
  • Without sufficient audit metadata, the status remains UNKNOWN for “exact” requests.
