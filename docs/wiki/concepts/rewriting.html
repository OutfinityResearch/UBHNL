Term rewriting & completion (Knuth–Bendix)

Methods for equalities and rewriting: confluence, critical pairs, and checkable proof traces.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki Certificate DS-011

Definitions

  • Term rewriting: rules l → r applied to transform terms toward a normal form.
  • Completion (Knuth–Bendix): attempts to turn a set of equalities into a convergent rewrite system.

In UBHNL

  • Modeled as an equality fragment (Frag_Eq) and/or FOL with equality (Frag_FOL_Eq).
  • Can be used as a backend/tactic for symbolic simplifications and lemma generation.

Certificate

Typical certificates include: joinability proofs for critical pairs and rewrite traces. The checker validates rewrite steps and any remaining obligations (possibly discharging sub-obligations via SMT/SAT).

Consequences

  • These methods are strong for equality-heavy problems, but they do not replace SAT/SMT for Boolean/numeric constraints.
  • In UBHNL, their role is modular (backend/tactic) and acceptance requires certificates.
