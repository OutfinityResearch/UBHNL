Fragment

“What kind of problem is this”: constraint classes + encodings + certificates.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki GoalKind Orchestrator DS-010

Definition

A fragment is a formalized class of constraints (sorts + operators + rules), together with encodings to other fragments and certificate expectations.

Why not “everything is UBH”

  • Some theories are more efficient natively (e.g., MIP/SDP/CAD) and then you verify certificates.
  • Some have different semantics (QBF: strategies), and certificates are not just “models”.
  • Fragment-first allows the orchestrator to choose coherent tactics and backends.

Engineering consequences

  • The semantic IR must retain enough structure to detect the fragment (not just “one giant CNF”).
  • Encodings must be defined as sound/equisatisfiable, with acceptance/certificate criteria.
