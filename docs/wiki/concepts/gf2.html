GF(2) (the Galois field with 2 elements)

The algebraic model behind XOR/AND.

DRAFT: Wiki page.
Wiki Theory UBH-SPEC DS-002

Definition

GF(2) is a field with two elements: {0,1}. Its operations are:

OperationIn GF(2)In UBH
additiona + b (mod 2)a ⊕ b (XOR)
multiplicationa · ba ∧ b (AND)

Why it matters in UBHNL

  • UBH expressions can be viewed as polynomials over GF(2), evaluated on Boolean inputs.
  • XOR constraints form a linear sub-fragment (GF(2) equations) that can be solved efficiently with Gauss.
  • AND introduces non-linearity (degree > 1 terms), typically requiring SAT/CDCL (or other methods).

Consequences

  • UBH design explicitly chooses this interpretation: the {XOR, AND, CONST} basis is minimal and functionally complete.
  • Hybrid solving: keep the “linear XOR part” and the “CNF part” separate for efficiency.
