UBH (Universal Boolean Hypergraph)

The minimal kernel: a hash-consed DAG of XOR/AND/CONST/VAR gates plus assertions.

DRAFT: this page describes the contract “as if the system were complete”. Normative contracts are in UBH-SPEC.
Wiki Theory UBH-SPEC DS-001

What UBH is

UBH is the bit-level substrate of UBHNL: a representation of Boolean expressions as a directed acyclic graph (DAG) of binary gates, with deduplication (hash-consing).

Primitive: the gate basis

ConstructorMeaningNotes
CONST00singleton
CONST11singleton
VAR(name)input wirekeyed by string
XOR(a,b)a ⊕ bcommutative, canonicalized
AND(a,b)a ∧ bcommutative, canonicalized

NOT is derived: NOT(x) := XOR(CONST1, x). Other operations (e.g., OR) are macros compiled into the basis above.

What UBH “solves”

UBH is not “a logic”; it is a minimal IR for many logics. The kernel receives Boolean constraints (assert) and answers solve()/prove() with SAT/UNSAT/UNKNOWN.

Semantics (in one sentence)

A model assigns values to all variables, each UBH node evaluates recursively using XOR/AND, and a theory is satisfiable if there exists a model that makes all assertions true.

Why this basis

  • Minimal (few primitives) and functionally complete for Boolean functions.
  • Algebraic (see GF(2)): XOR/AND have a natural interpretation as + and ·.
  • Efficient: XOR is handled natively as linear equations, while AND is encoded into CNF.
