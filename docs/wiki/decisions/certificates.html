Decision: mandatory certificates for acceptance

UNSAT/OPTIMAL without checkable evidence are not accepted; the system reports UNKNOWN.

DRAFT: decision page.
Wiki Certificate UNKNOWN DS-011

The decision

UBHNL has an explicit acceptance guard: results are accepted only if they are checkable. Otherwise the status is UNKNOWN (or “DISPROVED” with a witness for counterexamples).

Why

  • Real backends are complex and can have bugs; independent verification reduces risk.
  • It enables auditability: a result can be re-checked without the original solver.

Consequences

  • The trusted core must include checkers (DRAT/LRAT, invariants, dual certs, etc.).
  • The orchestrator must be able to “confirm” results through alternative routes when a backend does not produce a certificate.
