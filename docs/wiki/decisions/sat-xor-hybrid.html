Decision: hybrid solving with SAT + native XOR

Separates the linear part (XOR) from the non-linear part (AND/CNF) for efficiency and clarity.

DRAFT: decision page.
Wiki XOR linear SAT DS-002

The decision

UBHNL treats XOR constraints as GF(2) equations and uses an incremental Gaussian elimination engine, in cooperation with a SAT (CDCL) backend for the CNF part (AND via Tseitin).

Why

  • Encoding XOR into CNF can be expensive and can destroy propagation.
  • Incremental Gauss is very efficient for linear systems (common patterns in real circuits).
  • The linear/non-linear split mirrors GF(2) semantics: XOR=+, AND=Â·.

Consequences

  • You have two propagation channels and two conflict sources; the loop must be integrated (DPLL(T)-style).
  • Origins must be propagated in both worlds (clauses and equations) for unsat cores/explanations.
