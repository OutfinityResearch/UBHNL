Decision: DSL allows only a single @ per line

@ declares the statement target; $ references variables; bare names are vocabulary symbols.

DRAFT: decision page.
Wiki DSL DS-008

The decision

In DSL, the @name token is allowed at most once per line and it must be the first token. It represents the statement “subject/target”, not a reference.

Why

  • Syntactic clarity: separates “declare/define” from “reference”.
  • Simple parsing: avoids ambiguity and reduces special cases.
  • Graph model: makes the DSL naturally “subject-first” (properties/edges out of one node).

Consequences

  • Invalid example (intentional): @p0 likes @p1 (the second occurrence must be $p1 or a vocabulary constant).
  • Errors become local and actionable: the message can suggest the correct fix.
