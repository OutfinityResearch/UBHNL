What this enables

  • Explicit semantics: LLM proposals become CNL/DSL statements with a strict vocabulary.
  • Consistency checks: contradictions are detected, with proofs or counterexamples.
  • Explainability: outputs include origins and checkable evidence.
  • Safe tool usage: plans are validated against constraints before execution.

Typical flow with an LLM

  1. LLM drafts candidate rules or facts.
  2. UBHNL validates and normalizes them into CNL/DSL.
  3. Conflicts are reported (unsat cores), or accepted with proof/witness.
  4. LLM receives structured feedback and revises inputs.

Limits and constraints

  • LLM outputs must be translated into deterministic CNL; no ambiguity allowed.
  • Formal meaning is limited to the vocabulary and constraints defined by users.
  • Approximate results are labeled; strong claims require certificates.

What we still need

  • Interactive editors for CNL authoring and validation.
  • Feedback loops for LLM alignment with the vocabulary.
  • Metric suites for contradiction detection accuracy and coverage.
