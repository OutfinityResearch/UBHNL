Highly Regulated Industry

Formalize rules and detect contradictions across policies, procedures, and legal documents.

What this enables

  • Semantic normalization: translate policies and standards into CNL/DSL with explicit vocabulary.
  • Contradiction analysis: detect conflicts within a document and across multiple sources.
  • Audit-ready output: unsat cores and origins show which clauses caused a conflict.
  • Controlled updates: adding a rule is a change to a formal theory, not a free-text edit.

Workflow (practical)

  1. Define a regulated-domain vocabulary in Sys2 (roles, assets, permissions, constraints).
  2. Translate textual rules into CNL (assisted by tooling/LLMs, then validated).
  3. Load multiple documents as theory files; treat each as a named source.
  4. Run consistency checks and queries (prove/refute) across the combined theory.
  5. Export contradictions with origin references and certificate evidence.

Outputs you can expect

  • Explicit contradiction reports with traceable origins.
  • Coverage gaps: rules that are underspecified or conflicting.
  • Checkable acceptance: results are supported by certificates or a witness.

Limits and constraints

  • Ambiguous sentences are rejected; they must be rephrased into CNL.
  • All concepts must be modeled explicitly in the vocabulary.
  • Large domains require schema-based tactics; some results may be UNKNOWN under budget.

What we still need

  • Domain templates for common regulatory patterns (consent, retention, access control, reporting).
  • Guidelines for mapping legal text into CNL without semantic drift.
  • Certificate checkers for domain-specific backends (e.g., MIP, SMT).
