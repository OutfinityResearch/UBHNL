The Use Case: Managing Normative Contradictions

In highly regulated sectors—such as pharmaceuticals, finance, and aerospace—organizations must adhere to a multi-layered hierarchy of constraints: international standards, national laws, and internal operational procedures. A significant challenge arises when these sources, authored by different bodies at different times, contain latent contradictions or "semantic drift." For example, a data retention policy in a privacy regulation may clash with a record-keeping mandate in a financial statute, creating a legal impasse.

Traditional compliance management relies on manual cross-referencing, which is inherently prone to oversight. UBHNL addresses this by treating regulatory corpora as a unified logical theory. Every policy is modeled as a Formal Constraint rather than a descriptive text, enabling the system to exhaustively analyze the interplay between rules.

Methodology: Semantic Normalization and Lowering

The process begins with Semantic Normalization. Textual rules are mapped into Controlled Natural Language (CNL), which preserves the readability of the original rule while eliminating lexical ambiguity. This mapping ensures that terms are bound to a strict, pre-defined Lexicon, eliminating the risk of "equivocation" where the same word is used with different meanings across documents.

Once normalized, these rules are "lowered" into the UBH Kernel. In this Boolean substrate, the system performs a global consistency check. Unlike a simple keyword search or pattern match, the engine explores the entire combinatorial space of the rules to identify "conflict sets"—the minimal groups of statements that are mutually exclusive. This allows the organization to resolve contradictions at the design phase of a policy rather than during an audit.

Expected Results: Checkable Proofs and Auditability

The output of a UBHNL-enabled compliance check is a Verification Certificate. When a contradiction is detected, the system provides an "Unsat Core" that references the exact Origins of the conflicting rules. This allows auditors to trace a failure back to specific paragraphs in different regulatory documents.

This enables a new standard of auditability: Proof-Carrying Compliance. Decisions are no longer justified by expert opinion alone but are supported by checkable evidence that demonstrates their consistency within the established logical framework. This level of rigor is essential for automating risk assessment and ensuring safe operation in high-stakes environments.

Current Limitations and Research Directions

The system currently operates within the bounds of Exact Logic. While highly effective for identifying binary contradictions (e.g., conflicting data retention durations), it requires further expansion to handle the full complexity of legal reasoning:

  • Temporal Reasoning: Formalizing sequences of actions and deadlines over varying time horizons.
  • Deontic Logic Fragments: Distinguishing between strict prohibitions, mandatory obligations, and conditional permissions.
  • Automated Extraction: Enhancing the fidelity of the NLP pipelines that translate legacy legal text into deterministic CNL.
