The Epistemological Crisis: Coherence in the Age of Information Overload

The scientific community faces a growing challenge in maintaining theoretical coherence across an exponentially expanding body of literature. Discoveries in one sub-field often rely on assumptions that may be latent or contradicted by findings in another. Traditional peer review is limited by the cognitive bandwidth of human experts, who cannot exhaustively check a new hypothesis against every established constraint. UBHNL addresses this "Coherence Gap" by providing a formal language for Claim Encapsulation and automated verification.

Methodology: Scientific Theories as Constraint Networks

UBHNL enables researchers to represent hypotheses and empirical findings as sets of formal symbolic constraints. This transition from descriptive summaries to formal theories allows for several rigorous analytical processes:

Hypothesis Validation: Researchers can translate a new hypothesis into CNL and query the system to verify if it is logically compatible with existing experimental results. If the solver returns UNSAT, it provides a minimal conflict set (Unsat Core), identifying the specific established laws or data points that the new hypothesis contradicts.

Automated "Red-Teaming" of Papers: Peer reviewers can use the system to identify hidden assumptions or non-obvious edge cases where a proposed model fails. By searching for counterexamples (witnesses of inconsistency), the system acts as a formal "stress test" for scientific theories.

Global Dependency Mapping: Because every rule in UBHNL carries an Origin, the system can generate a global graph of scientific dependencies. This illustrates which conclusions rely on which specific axioms, allowing the community to track how a revision in a foundational theory propagates through all dependent research.

Outcome: The "Proof-Carrying" Research Paper

The ultimate objective is the development of Executable Scientific Manuscripts. In this vision, a paper is not just a PDF, but a formal theory bundle (DSL/CNL). This allows the research to be reproducible by construction: any external observer can run the formal model, verify its internal consistency, and check the validity of its claims using the trusted UBH Kernel.

Research Frontier: Integrating Uncertainty and Magnitude
