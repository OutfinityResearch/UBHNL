AGISystem2 (UBH NL) Structural Logic

Utilizing constraint satisfaction to verify narrative consistency and track structural invariants in complex storylines.

The Analytical Objective: Ensuring Narrative Coherence

Complex fictional structures—including long-form novels, cinematic franchises, and branching game narratives—face a significant risk of logical decay. As characters, locations, and rules proliferate, it becomes increasingly difficult for human authors or editors to track the consistency of causality, timeline, and character knowledge. UBHNL addresses this by applying Formal Narrative Verification, treating the story's facts and universal rules as a logical theory.

Methodology: Story Elements as Formal Invariants

The analysis begins with the definition of a Narrative Lexicon (e.g., characters, locations, magical or technological systems, and time points). Mapping story events to CNL/DSL enables several advanced verification workflows:

Causal and Timeline Integrity: The system verifies that the sequence of events adheres to fundamental invariants. For example, it can prove if a character's presence in a specific scene contradicts their previously established location or if a plot resolution relies on a mechanism that violates the "hard rules" of the setting.

Epistemic Consistency: UBHNL can track "what characters know" at any given point. This prevents "epistemic hallucinations"—where a character acts on information they have not yet acquired—thereby identifying subtle "plot holes" that would be non-obvious during a standard reading.

Stylistic and Structural Rule Enforcement: Authors can encode stylistic constraints (e.g., POV restrictions, tense consistency, or thematic repetition limits) as formal rules. The system can then query the narrative to identify passages that violate these "soft" or "hard" structural constraints.

Workflow: Solving for Narrative Contradictions

When a narrative theory is formalized, UBHNL can be queried for Satisfiability. If the narrative is consistent, the system can generate witnesses—alternative satisfying interpretations of ambiguous scenes. If inconsistent (UNSAT), the solver identifies the Unsat Core—the minimal set of plot points and rules that are mutually exclusive—allowing editors to resolve the contradiction at its source.

Research Frontier: Probabilistic and Multi-Threaded Logic

Literature often thrives on ambiguity and "unreliable narrators." We are extending the UBHNL framework to handle Probabilistic Story Threads. This will allow the system to model multiple potential plot outcomes, each with an associated weight, while maintaining formal consistency within each branch. Furthermore, we are developing authoring tools that assist in the extraction of logical structures from natural language prose without infringing on the creative process.
