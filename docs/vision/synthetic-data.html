AGISystem2 (UBH NL) Constraint-Driven Synthesis

Generating datasets that are guaranteed to satisfy complex logical and probabilistic invariants.

The Fidelity Challenge: Why Statistical Mimicry is Insufficient

Mainstream synthetic data generation relies on Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) or Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) to mimic the statistical distribution of real datasets. While effective for visual or broad linguistic patterns, these neural models frequently generate "semantically invalid" data—records that are statistically likely but logically impossible (e.g., a medical record showing a male patient with a pregnancy-related diagnosis). In regulated sectors like finance and medicine, these logical failures render the data useless for testing or training high-integrity systems.

The Solution: Synthesis via Constraint Satisfaction

UBHNL approaches data generation as a Formal Synthesis Task. Instead of learning from a dataset, the system generates data from a Theory. By defining the domain schema and invariants as formal constraints, UBHNL ensures that every generated record is logically sound.

Hard Logical Invariants: Data is generated using a Witness Search. The system searches for model assignments that satisfy the entire set of rules (e.g., "Transaction date must be within 24 hours of account opening"). If a record exists, the system finds it; if no such record is logically possible, the system proves UNSAT.

Probabilistic Fidelity: To maintain statistical utility, UBHNL utilizes its probabilistic reasoning fragments. Users can attach weights to specific rules or domains, guiding the distribution of the generated data while the SAT kernel ensures that no hard logic is ever violated.

Traceable Data Provenance: Every generated record is accompanied by a formal trace. This trace identifies exactly which business rules the record satisfies, providing a level of auditability that is impossible with "black-box" neural generators.

Methodology: Systematic Solution-Space Exploration

The Orchestrator builds a plan to sample from the satisfying assignments of the theory. This moves beyond random generation toward Formal Coverage Testing. The system can be queried to generate "maximal diversity" samples—records that lie at the boundaries of the rule-set—which are often the most valuable for training robust models and identifying hidden bugs in operational code.

Research Horizons: Large-Scale Sampling Backends

The primary challenge in formal synthesis is the computational cost of generating millions of satisfying witnesses. We are optimizing Probabilistic SAT Backends specifically for high-throughput sampling. Future iterations will support "Incremental Synthesis," allowing for the rapid generation of new records that maintain consistency with previously generated data while adhering to global statistical targets.
