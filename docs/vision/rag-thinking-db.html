AGISystem2 (UBH NL) Symbolic RAG

Enhancing Retrieval-Augmented Generation through formal consistency gates and active symbolic memory.

The Epistemological Challenge: The Stochastic Retrieval Gap

Traditional Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems operate primarily at the level of semantic proximity. By utilizing embedding similarity, they retrieve document chunks that are linguistically related to a query. However, linguistic similarity is not a proxy for logical consistency. A traditional RAG system can easily retrieve mutually exclusive facts from different sources and present them as a unified answer, leading to "knowledge hallucinations" where the error resides not in the model's generation, but in the lack of symbolic validation of the retrieved context.

The Solution: Thinking Databases and Active Memory

UBHNL introduces the concept of a Thinking Database—a repository that treats stored information as an active, consistent logical theory rather than inert text. This transition enables several advanced cognitive capabilities:

Active Consistency Checking: As facts are retrieved, the UBHNL engine evaluates them against a core theory. If a retrieved artifact contradicts a foundational invariant or a previously accepted fact, the system identifies the conflict before generation. This "Consistency Gate" prevents the LLM from synthesizing contradictory information.

Implicational Retrieval: Standard databases answer "What does the text say?". A Thinking Database answers "What must be true given the text?". By reasoning over the formal layer, UBHNL can retrieve not just explicit mentions, but derived consequences that are logically entailed by the stored rules.

Provenance and Auditability: Every synthesized conclusion is tethered to its formal Origin. Instead of a general citation, UBHNL provides a specific trace showing exactly which rules and facts from which documents were combined to produce the result, accompanied by a checkable witness model.

Architecture: The Hybrid Indexing Pipeline

The ingestion process for a Thinking Database involves a Formal Normalization Pipeline. Documents are passed through extraction gates that translate candidate facts into CNL/DSL. These statements are then stored in a dual-indexed structure:

  • Semantic Index: For rapid, proximity-based discovery.
  • Symbolic Index: For formal reasoning, constraint satisfaction, and contradiction detection.
This hybrid approach allows the system to discover relevant information through semantic similarity and then "upgrade" that discovery into a formal reasoning plan managed by the Orchestrator.

Future Research: Incremental Reasoning at Scale

Scaling Thinking Databases to millions of constraints requires Incremental Logic Maintenance. We are developing techniques for "Reasoning-Aware Caching," where the system tracks the impact of new facts on the consistency of the entire database without performing a full global re-solve. This enables the database to remain performant during high-velocity ingestion while maintaining its absolute logical integrity.
